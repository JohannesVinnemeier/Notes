 \subsection{Changing the fiber}

     Given a fiber bundle, Theorem \ref{fiber_bundles:thm:fiber_bundle_construction1} allows us to change the fiber:

    \begin{corollary}[Changing the fiber]
        \label{fiber_bundles:cor:changing_fiber}
        Let $F \hookrightarrow E \oset[0.6pt]{\scalebox{0.8}{$p$}}{\longrightarrow} B$ be a fiber bundle with structure group $G$. Given a space $F^\prime$ upon which $G$ acts effectively, there exists a unique (up to isomorphism) fiber bundle 
        \[
        F^\prime \hookrightarrow E^\prime \oset[3pt]{\scalebox{0.8}{$p^\prime$}}{\longrightarrow} B
        \]
        which has the same trivialising neighbourhoods $(U_\alpha)$ and the same transition functions $(\tau_{\alpha,\beta})$ as $F \hookrightarrow E \oset[0.6pt]{\scalebox{0.8}{$p$}}{\longrightarrow} B$. We denote this bundle by $E \times_G F^\prime$ (see Lemma \ref{fiber_bundles:lem:borel_construction} for the origin of this notation).
    \end{corollary}

    Theorem \ref{fiber_bundles:thm:fiber_bundle_construction1} tells us, that the new bundle still retains all of the information of the old bundle, as it is uniquely determined by the transition functions, i.e. we can simply change the fiber again to get the original bundle back. 

    \begin{example}
         Given any fiber bundle $F \hookrightarrow E \oset[0.6pt]{\scalebox{0.8}{$p$}}{\longrightarrow} B$ with structure group $G$ we can apply the above corollary with $F^\prime := G$ upon which $G$ acts effectively by left-translations to get a principal $G$-bundle over $B$. This bundle is called the \textbf{underlying principal bundle} of $E \to B$ (see Definition \ref{fiber_bundles:def:principal_bundle}).
    \end{example}

    \begin{example}
        Using the above corollary we can also construct the frame bundle from Example \ref{fiber_bundles:example:frame_bundle} more abstractly by defining it as the underlying principal bundle of $E \to F$. Note that the uniqueness results in Theorem \ref{fiber_bundles:thm:fiber_bundle_construction1} shows, that the bundle constructed in this way is in fact isomorphic to $\mathrm{GL}(E)$. The construction in Example \ref{fiber_bundles:example:frame_bundle} of course has the benefit of being more concrete.
    \end{example}

    \needspace{4cm}

    We can use Proposition \ref{fiber_bundles:prop:construction_of_morphisms} to get a much stronger form of Corollary \ref{fiber_bundles:cor:changing_fiber}:

    \begin{theorem}[The Change of Fiber Functor]
        \label{fiber_bundles:thm:change_of_fiber_functor}
        Let $G$ be a topological group acting effectively on spaces $F$ and $F^\prime$. Then changing the fiber from $F$ to $F^\prime$ defines a functor 
        \[
        \times_G F^\prime \colon \mathsf{Fib}_{F,G} \to \mathsf{Fib}_{F^\prime,G}.
        \]
        Moreover the composition $\times_G F \circ \times_G F^\prime$ is naturally isomorphic to the identity on $\mathsf{Fib}_{F,G}$. Thus $\times_G F$ defines an equivalence of categories.
    \end{theorem}

    \begin{proof}
    The proof is straightforward but takes some care to write down precisely. For every bundle $E$ in $\mathsf{Fib}_{F,G}$ choose a bundle $E \times_G F^\prime$ in $\mathsf{Fib}_{F^\prime,G}$ with the same trivialising neighbourhoods and transition functions as $E$ (using Corollary \ref{fiber_bundles:cor:changing_fiber}). On morphisms we define the functor as follows. Let 
    \[\begin{tikzcd}
        E_1 & {E_2} \\
        B_1 & {B_1}
        \arrow["H_1", from=1-1, to=1-2]
        \arrow[from=1-1, to=2-1]
        \arrow[from=1-2, to=2-2]
        \arrow["h_1", from=2-1, to=2-2]
        \end{tikzcd}\]
    be a $G$-morphism in $\mathsf{Fib}_{F,G}$ . Choose a trivialising cover $(U_\alpha)$ of $E_1$ and $(V_\beta)$ of $E_2$, such that for all $\alpha$ there exists $\beta$ with $h_1(U_\alpha) \subseteq V_\beta$. Then in local trivialisations $H$ is given by 
    \[
        U_\alpha \times F \to V_\beta \times F, \; \; (x,f) \mapsto (h_1(x), \, g_{\alpha\beta}(x) \cdot f)
    \]
    for some $g_{\alpha\beta} \colon U_\alpha \to G$, which transform as required in Proposition \ref{fiber_bundles:prop:construction_of_morphisms}. Since by definition $(U_\alpha)$ is also a trivialising cover of $E_1 \times_G F^\prime$ with the same transition functions as $E_1$ and similiarly for $E_2 \times_G F^\prime$, we can apply Proposition \ref{fiber_bundles:prop:construction_of_morphisms} to construct a $G$-morphism $K \colon E_1 \times_G F^\prime \to E_2 \times_G F^\prime$. We claim that $K$ does not depend on the chosen covers $(U_\alpha)$ and $(V_\beta)$. So let $(U^\prime_{\alpha^\prime})$ and $(V^\prime_{\beta^\prime})$ be different trivialising covers with the same property and define $K^\prime \colon E_1 \times_G F^\prime \to E_2 \times_G F^\prime$ in the same way. Then we can also use the covers $(U_\alpha) \cup (U^\prime_{\alpha^\prime})$ and $(V_\beta) \cup (V^\prime_{\beta^\prime})$ to construct a $G$-morphism $K^{\prime\prime} \colon E_1 \times_G F^\prime \to E_2 \times_G F^\prime$, which by definition agrees with $K$ on $E_1|_{U_\alpha}$ for all $\alpha$, such that $h_1(U_\alpha) \subseteq V_\beta$ for some $\beta$ and similiarly for $K^\prime$. Thus $K = K^{\prime\prime} = K^\prime$. We may then define 
    \[
    H_1 \times_G F^\prime := K.
    \]
    To see that this is functorial let 
    \[\begin{tikzcd}
        E_2 & {E_3} \\
        B_2 & {B_3}
        \arrow["H_2", from=1-1, to=1-2]
        \arrow[from=1-1, to=2-1]
        \arrow[from=1-2, to=2-2]
        \arrow["h_2", from=2-1, to=2-2]
        \end{tikzcd}\]
    be another $G$-morphism. Let $(U_\alpha)$,$(V_\beta)$ and $(W_\gamma)$ be trivialising covers of $E_1$,$E_2$ and $E_3$ respectively, such that for all $\alpha$ there exists $\beta$ with $h_1(U_\alpha) \subseteq V_\beta$ and for all $\beta$ there exists $\gamma$ with $h_2(V_\beta) \subseteq W_\gamma$. Then the covers $(U_\alpha)$ and $(W_\gamma)$ may be used to construct $(H_2 \circ H_1) \times_G F^\prime$ as above and for $\alpha,\beta,\gamma$ with $h_1(U_\alpha) \subseteq V_\beta$ and $h_2(V_\beta) \subseteq W_\gamma$ this map clearly agrees with the composition $H_2 \times_G F^\prime \circ H_1 \times_G F^\prime$. Moreover it is clear that the identity gets mapped to the identity under $ \times_G F^\prime$ by using $(V_\beta) = (U_\alpha)$ for the construction. 
    
    Next we show the statement about the equivalence of categories. First we define a canonical isomorphism
    \[
    \eta_E \colon E \to (E \times_G F^\prime) \times_G F
    \]
    as follows. Let $(U_\alpha)$ be a trivialising cover for $E$ with transition functions $\tau_{\beta\alpha} \colon U_\alpha \cap U_\beta \to G$. Then by definition $(U_\alpha)$ is also a trivialising cover for $(E \times_G F^\prime) \times_G F$ with the same transition functions. For $\alpha,\beta$ with $U_\alpha \subseteq U_\beta$ set $G_{\alpha\beta} := \tau_{\beta\alpha}$ (the reversed order of $\alpha$ and $\beta$ only has to do with the notational conventions used for the transition functions). Then these transform as in Proposition \ref{fiber_bundles:prop:construction_of_morphisms}, and thus define a $G$-morphism $\eta_E \colon E \to (E \times_G F^\prime) \times_G F$. This is an isomorphism, since it corresponds to the identity
    \[
    U_\alpha \times F \to  U_\alpha \times F
    \]
    under local trivialisations and as before we see that $\eta_E$ does not depend on the cover $(U_\alpha)$. Finally for the naturality let $H_1 \colon E_1 \to E_2$ be as above and again choose trivialising covers $(U_\alpha)$ of $E_1$ and $(V_\beta)$ of $E_2$, such that for all $\alpha$ there exists $\beta$ with $h_1(U_\alpha) \subseteq V_\beta$. Then for fixed $\alpha, \beta$ with $h_1(U_\alpha) \subseteq V_\beta$ the maps $H_1$ and $(H_1 \times_G F^\prime) \times_G F$ both correspond to the same map 
    \[
    U_\alpha \times F \to V_\beta \times F.
    \]
    Moreover since $\eta_{E_1}$ and $\eta_{E_2}$ correspond to the identity maps on $U_\alpha \times F$ and $V_\beta \times F$, respectively, the statement follows. 
    \end{proof}

    Changing the fiber satisifes the following naturality condition with respect to the pullback operation. 
    
    \begin{proposition}
        \label{fiber_bundles:prop:natruality_of_changing_fiber}
        Let $F \hookrightarrow E \oset[0.6pt]{\scalebox{0.8}{$p$}}{\longrightarrow} B$ be a fiber bundle with structure group $G$,$F^\prime$ a space upon which $G$ acts effectively and $f \colon B^\prime \to B$ a continuous map. Then 
        \[
        (f^\ast E) \times_G F^\prime \cong f^\ast(E \times_G F^\prime).
        \]
    \end{proposition}

    \begin{proof}
        For the sake of brevity let us call an open cover $(U_\alpha)$ of some base space $X$ of a fiber bundle $Q \to X$ a trivialising cover for $Q$, if the $U_\alpha$ are trivialising neighbourhoods for $Q$. Now let $(U_\alpha)$ be a trivialising cover for $E$ with transition functions $\tau_{\alpha,\beta} \colon U_\alpha \cap U_\beta \to G$. Then by definition $(f^{-1}(U_\alpha))$ is a trivialising cover for $f^\ast E$ with transition functions $$\tau_{\alpha,\beta}\circ f \colon f^{-1}(U_\alpha) \cap f^{-1}(U_\beta) \to G.$$ Thus again by definition $(f^{-1}(U_\alpha))$ is a trivialising cover of $(f^\ast E) \times_G F^\prime$ with transition functions $\tau_{\alpha,\beta}\circ f$. Similiarly we see that this same cover is a trivialising cover for $f^\ast(E \times_G F^\prime)$ with $\tau_{\alpha,\beta}\circ f$ as transition functions. Thus the statement follows from the uniqueness result of Theorem \ref{fiber_bundles:thm:fiber_bundle_construction1}.
    \end{proof}

    More abstractly we can formulate this propostion as follows. Let $\mathcal{F}_{F,G}(B)$ denote the set of isomorphism classes of fiber bundles with fiber $F$ and structure group $G$ over a given base space $B$. Then $\mathcal{F}_{F,G}(-)$ defines a contravariant functor $\mathsf{Top} \to \mathsf{Set}$ and changing the fiber defines a natural isomorphism of functors $\mathcal{F}_{F,G}(-) \Rightarrow \mathcal{F}_{F^\prime,G}(-)$.
